# Scoring Model

## Purpose

The Project Intelligence Score provides a standardized method for comparing infrastructure opportunities.

The objective is not to replace engineering studies.

The objective is to identify where detailed studies should be performed.

---

# Scoring Philosophy

OIIP is a de-risking platform.

Scores represent relative attractiveness and readiness.

Higher scores indicate lower uncertainty and stronger project fundamentals.

---

# Score Range

All scores use:

```text
0 – 100
```

Interpretation:

| Score  | Interpretation         |
| ------ | ---------------------- |
| 0-20   | Not Recommended        |
| 21-40  | High Risk              |
| 41-60  | Requires Further Study |
| 61-80  | Promising              |
| 81-100 | Strong Candidate       |

---

# Component Scores

## Resource Score

Measures resource availability.

Inputs:

* Thermal gradient
* Ocean depth
* Distance to deep water

Weight:

```text
25%
```

---

## Climate Score

Measures survivability and resilience.

Inputs:

* Cyclones
* Wave climate
* Currents
* Seismic activity

Weight:

```text
20%
```

---

## Economic Score

Measures economic viability.

Inputs:

* Electricity prices
* Diesel displacement potential
* Demand profile
* Energy security impact

Weight:

```text
20%
```

---

## Infrastructure Score

Measures deployment readiness.

Inputs:

* Ports
* Grid connectivity
* Submarine cables
* Logistics

Weight:

```text
15%
```

---

## Policy Score

Measures regulatory readiness.

Inputs:

* Energy policy
* Permitting complexity
* Incentives
* PPP frameworks

Weight:

```text
10%
```

---

## Nexus Score

Measures co-benefit potential.

Inputs:

* Desalination
* Aquaculture
* Hydrogen
* Datacenter cooling

Weight:

```text
5%
```

---

## Bankability Score

Measures financing readiness.

Inputs:

* Country risk
* Project maturity
* Market conditions

Weight:

```text
5%
```

---

# Project Intelligence Score

The overall score combines all component scores.

```text
Project Intelligence Score

=
25% Resource
+
20% Climate
+
20% Economic
+
15% Infrastructure
+
10% Policy
+
5% Nexus
+
5% Bankability
```

---

# MVP Scope

Version 1 implements:

* Resource Score
* Climate Score
* Economic Score

The remaining scores are introduced in future releases.

---

# Guiding Principle

A score must never be treated as a final investment recommendation.

Scores exist to guide attention, prioritize opportunities, and reduce uncertainty during early-stage project development.
