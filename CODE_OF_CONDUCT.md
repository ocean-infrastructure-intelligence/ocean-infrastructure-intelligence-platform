# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as contributors, maintainers, and community members pledge to make participation in the Ocean Infrastructure Intelligence Platform (OIIP) community a respectful, welcoming, and harassment-free experience for everyone.

We are committed to fostering an open, inclusive, and collaborative environment regardless of:

* Age
* Body size
* Disability
* Ethnicity
* Gender identity or expression
* Level of experience
* Nationality
* Personal appearance
* Race
* Religion
* Sexual identity and orientation
* Technical background
* Academic affiliation

Our community values scientific integrity, constructive collaboration, and respectful communication.

---

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment include:

* Being respectful and professional
* Demonstrating empathy toward others
* Welcoming newcomers
* Sharing knowledge openly
* Giving and receiving constructive feedback
* Focusing on evidence-based discussion
* Respecting different viewpoints and experiences
* Supporting collaborative problem-solving
* Crediting the work of others appropriately

---

## Examples of Unacceptable Behavior

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

* Harassment of any kind
* Personal attacks
* Trolling or insulting comments
* Deliberate intimidation
* Discriminatory remarks
* Publishing private information without permission
* Threatening language
* Sustained disruptive behavior
* Misrepresentation of research results
* Deliberate spreading of misinformation
* Plagiarism or failure to provide proper attribution

---

## Scientific Integrity

OIIP is both a software and research initiative.

Contributors are expected to:

* Clearly distinguish facts from opinions
* Cite sources whenever possible
* Document assumptions and methodologies
* Avoid intentionally misleading interpretations
* Respect intellectual property rights
* Promote reproducible research practices

Scientific disagreements are welcome when conducted respectfully and supported by evidence.

---

## Community Responsibilities

Project maintainers are responsible for:

* Clarifying and enforcing community standards
* Acting fairly and consistently
* Reviewing reports of misconduct
* Protecting contributors from harassment
* Maintaining a constructive environment

Maintainers may remove, edit, or reject contributions that violate this Code of Conduct.

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to:

* GitHub repositories
* Issues
* Pull Requests
* Discussions
* Project documentation
* Community communications
* Official project events
* Any public spaces where an individual represents the project

---

## Reporting Issues

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, please report it to the project maintainers.

When reporting an incident, include:

* Description of the issue
* Relevant links or screenshots
* Date and time of occurrence
* Any additional context

All reports will be reviewed confidentially and in good faith.

---

## Enforcement Guidelines

Community leaders may take any action they deem appropriate, including:

### Correction

A private warning explaining the violation and expected behavior.

### Warning

A formal warning regarding continued inappropriate behavior.

### Temporary Restriction

Temporary suspension from project interactions.

### Permanent Ban

Permanent removal from project participation for severe or repeated violations.
